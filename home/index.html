A Letter from the Secretary-General

Dear Delegates and Faculty Advisors,

The eleventh session of Hwa Chong Conflict Resolution and Inquiry will be held from 30 May to 1 June 2022 in an entirely virtual format.

Established in 2011, HCCRI has evolved from its humble beginnings into Singapore’s leading and most prestigious Model United Nations (MUN) conference in the pre-university circuit. We bring together Singapore’s brightest and most promising young leaders around the field of international relations and expose them to the sophisticated policy-making processes on the international level through the MUN framework.

HCCRI prides itself on its innovative, dynamic and academically rigorous simulations, inspiring thoughtful discourse and challenging debate. HCCRI is helmed by experienced Secretariat and Student Officers dedicated to guiding prospective diplomats through the often tumultuous world of diplomacy.

The Secretariat is delighted to announce that HCCRI is accepting delegate registration from participating schools. Our conference prospectus, application instructions, committees and deadlines will be found under the “registration” tab on our website. Because we believe in equitable access to MUN opportunities, HCCRI XI is committed to creating a diverse environment for debate and networking, and will not be collecting registration fees.

We look forward to upholding the unparalleled level of academic excellence that HCCRI is known for at our eleventh session.

Sincerely,
Wong Zhi Xuan
Secretary-General

Guest-of-Honour

Mr. Steven Okun
Founder and Chief Executive Officer, APAC Advisors
Senior Advisor, McLarty Associates

Education
B.A. (University of Virginia)
J.D. (University of Virginia School of Law)

A leader of corporate public affairs, sustainability and stakeholder engagement in Asia Pacific, Mr. Okun boasts over 25 years of experience across government, public affairs and communications. His illustrious career in the corporate world include his leadership of APAC public affairs in global investment firm KKR and logistics firm UPS, during the former of which he directed KKR’s Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) policies, in particular its impact investing pro bono programme, in the region from 2011 to 2017. Instrumental in securing UPS Airlines’ authority to operate in China in 2001, he subsequently oversaw UPS’ APAC public affairs matters on aviation, competition and trade. Additionally, Mr Okun has held key leadership positions in numerous business chambers and international associations, including serving three terms as the Chairman of both the American Chamber of Commerce in Singapore and the Asia Pacific Council of American Chambers of Commerce, both of which he currently serves on the board of.

Aside from his corporate experience, Mr. Okun served in the Clinton administration as Deputy General Counsel at the U.S. Department of Transportation, for which he was awarded the Secretary’s Award for Special Service Commendation. Advocating the views of the U.S. business community in APAC, he is a periodic guest host for CNBC and Channel NewsAsia and authors commentaries for The Straits Times. Owing to his extensive experience in a breadth of fields, Mr. Okun is a highly sought-after speaker on U.S. politics, foreign policy and their connections to business, with an increasing focus on the pertinent topic of sustainability.

About APAC Advisors

Operating at the intersection of business, finance, government and sustainability, APAC Advisors empowers clients in the private capital sector to drive initiatives delivering value-add impact via partnership with stakeholders including government, employees, customers, investors, associations, media and non-governmental organisations. With core services encompassing government relations, public policy, ESG initiatives and sustainability, APAC Advisors advises clients ranging from global corporations to private equity firms and NGOs.

APAC Advisors has a strategic partnership to deepen the APAC presence of international geostrategic consultancy McLarty Associates. In addition, the firm is appointed as the ASEAN Representative for EMPEA, a global industry association for private capital in emerging markets.
